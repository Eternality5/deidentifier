for de-identification of data.

Overview

The framework interacts with users to gather information about the data and its attributes without directly accessing the actual data. We have designed four sets of questions to collect the necessary details. Below is the description of the sets and the questions within each set:

Set-1: Data Inventory

The first set of questions helps in identifying the types of data and the data fields that are present in the dataset.

Questions

  1. Do any fields contain unique identifiers?
  2. Please select all the geographic attributes present in dataset?
  3. Please select all the time related information present in dataset?
  4. Please select all the information related to financial transactions present in dataset?
  5. Please select all the demographic information present in dataset?
  6. Are there any free-text or narrative fields?

Outcomes

  • Categorize the data attributes based on the information provided by the user.
  • Identify the most sensitive attributes (identifiers) that need to be protected.
  • Assess the potential risk of re-identification based on combinations of attributes.
  • Use this information to determine the appropriate de-identification techniques for each attribute.

Set-2: Persecutor Risk

The following questions are relating to finding if the persecutor risk is applicable to the dataset. A persecutor risk is when the a person (the persecutor) is trying to identify specific individual that they know is in the dataset. The persecutor already has information about the person they are attempting to identify.

Questions

  1. The dataset represents the whole population (e.g., a population registry, or census) or has a large sampling fraction?
  2. It generally know who participated in the study, such as an internal survey for a single business?
  3. The individuals are likely to self-reveal that they are part of the sample? For example, subjects in clinical trials do generally inform their family, friends, and even acquaintances that they are participating in a trial.

Outcomes

  • Helps assesses whether the dataset covers the entire population or a significant portion of it. If it does, it implies that the dataset may contain more comprehensive and identifiable information about individuals.
  • Raises awareness about the participants in the dataset and if there is a clear record of who took part, it increases the likelihood of identifying individuals.
  • Assess the potential risk of re-identification based on combinations of attributes.
  • Whether individuals are likely to disclose their participation in the dataset to others, such self-disclosure can impact the privacy and anonymity of the dataset.

Set-3: K-Anonymity

The following questions will be used to to determine if the characteristcs of their dataset that are related to K-Anonymity.

Questions

  1. Is your given dataset a subset of a larger dataset?
  2. What is the current K-Annonimity of the dataset?
  3. What is the K-Anonymity of the dataset when combined with the larger dataset?

Outcomes

  • Understanding the dataset's relationship with a larger dataset one can be essential in assessing K-Anonymity.
  • The K-Anonymity of the dataset in its current state. It helps assess the level of privacy protection in the dataset.
  • Assess the potential risk of re-identification based on combinations of attributes.
  • The K-Anonymity of the provided dataset when integrated with the larger dataset and helps assessing the impact of combining datasets on privacy.
